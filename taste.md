# What Makes a Good Paper Recommendation

It is very important to have good taste in paper selection. There are hundreds of papers published everyday, only a few are worth attention. The most important criterion to optimize for in curating papers is helping me succeed in my research. What does this mean? It means sometimes you might find a paper that is super relevant to what I'm thinking about and suggest that. A paper might have just come out that solves a related problem. A paper might apply a related technique to a different area. You might find an old paper that may offer guidance and inspiration. A paper may obviate my line of thinking.

Another way to look at it is to consider the reasons why a paper might be good. There are three reasons:
- A paper may introduce an interesting or useful problem, or be the first one to provide a solution to an existing problem.
- A paper may be the "last" in a certain problem, meaning that it solves the problem so completely that nothing more needs to be done.
- A paper may offer a useful shift in perspective, it might collate prior approaches into a unifying framework and give a better solution than others.

These points are a high bar, but useful to keep in mind.
